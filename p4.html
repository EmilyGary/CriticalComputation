Exquisite Corpse - Tax Season

Description

This project is based on the surrealist technique "exquisite corpse", where participants alternate drawing different parts of a figure without seeing what the others have drawn. We borrowed this same technique to create an exquisite corpse by combining the head, body, and legs of the figures drawn by three other classmates.

The Scene

Oh no! How could this have happened? And right before the meeting about the audit. Barry was suddenly panicked as he looked at his coworkers Panic Pete and Fashion Robot. All of their bodies had somehow gotten jumbled after they were using the new copy machine. There was no way Barry could go to his meeting wearing Fashion Robot's flamboyant shoes, and he especially could not pull off Panic Pete's shirtless look.

Panic Pete shrugged it off, he didn't much care to worry about his own problems. Barry hurriedly put his shirt on over his new, squishier torso and begged Fashion Robot for a more reasonable set of shoes. They said they had extras at their desk, but were disappointed by the overall look Barry was sporting. Fashion Robot expressed concern that people would recognize their legs and this look would ruin their hard-earned reputation. After much arguing, Fashion Robot conceded to the black "office shoes" they kept at their desk for emergencies. Barry was in such a hurry to get to the audit that Fashion Robot intentionally neglected to tell him about the left shoe, which remained bright red. Barry wasn't used to changing eight shoes at once so it was easy to forget one. He was flushed red, so Fashion Robot put a little makeup on him so he would appear more collected. He hurried down the hallway to his meeting, hoping that no one would notice his strange appearance.

Design Process

The Characters

The three characters drawn by the other participants were Barry, Fashion Robot, and Panic Pete. Their pictures and descriptions are provided below:

Barry (Head)
by Franco Chen

This is Barry, your personal accountant. He filed your taxes incorrectly this year and because of him, the IRS is performing an audit on you. Barry doesn’t seem to mind, and he blames the audit on your excessive spending. You’re no accountant, but you think Barry is full of it.

Panic Pete (Body)
by Alex Modlin

Panic Pete is a kind enough sort. He has a flesh-colored, slightly bulbous body with blue balls for eyes and red balls for ears as well as nose. His body has some faint details of a clown outfit, but he doesn’t speak of this chapter in his life. It’s his business alone. Panic Pete is a wanderer now. He helps those dealing with their insecurities and core miseries release their tensions by allowing them to squeeze him. That is the power he has. He is Panic Pete.

Fashion Robot (Legs)
by Rachel Gorman

Fashion Robot is extra extra and that’s just how it likes it. It’s built to wear multiple sunglasses at a time. It has no arms, but a body made for draping capes. It has tons of feet so that it never has to choose between shoe options. It’s got two sets of ears for taking in all of your compliments, but it is always green with envy because it can’t wear your fabulous lewk along with its own.
