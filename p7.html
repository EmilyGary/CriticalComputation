Casi Madrileños

Click and use the arrow keys to join Hans and Emily on their new adventure in Madrid.

Description

This game is an autobiographical depiction of moving to Madrid, Spain with my dog, Hans. The game is meant to detail the struggles of being in a new place and the unexpected issues that arrise as you adjust to living in a new country.

Design Process

When I moved to Madrid, Hans was the only creature I knew. With that, I decided to make him the protagonist of the game. Though there were many obstacles large and small as I adjused to my new home, I chose to integrate the smaller details into my game to make it more whimsical and preserve some of the smaller details of my memories in Madrid that I might otherwise forget.
I formatted the game in a Pacman format, with certain objects being good and others being bad. This choice allowed me to tell more of my story by incorporating the challenges and rewards into each scene. Initially, I wanted to add layers of complexity to the game, with different objects having "special" behaviors such as attractive forces, however after playing I realized that this added complexity without contributing anything specific to the overall game dynamics. I chose to use real photographs from Madrid as the backdrops for each scene to play up my nostalgia, and I thought that the combination of real photos with pixelated caricatures added to the ridiculousness of some of the scenes. Reflection

This game was a huge learning experience implementing object interactions, which has a lot more intricacy than I had initially thought. I also learned a lot about my own design process. I am much more efficient when I sketch out my ideas by hand before starting to code. Seeing the storyboards on paper helps me to quickly see what will work and what won't, and also helps me quickly decide which ideas I should move forward with. I also learned that I need to allocate more time for the visuals, since creating various objects can be quite time-consuming, particularly when I am trying to have a cohesive style throughout my game.
