Optical Illusion

Click to reset the sketch!

Description

This project is a nod to Seattle, which I consider my adult home after living in several places throughout my 20's. The project is inspired by the wildflowers of Mount Rainier shown in the photo below.

Design Process

I chose to use a pattern from nature rather than a textile pattern for this project. The randomness of the placement is meant to reflect that seen in nature and I decided to let the pattern continue on in perpetuity to add a playfulness to the design.

Reflection

There is irony in translating a pattern from nature into digital form, which is representative of the current tumultous relationship between people, technology, and nature. The original photograph which inspired this design was taken at Mount Rainer National Park, unplugged from the technology that is constantly begging for our attention. The animation recontextualizes the pattern to fit it into our contemporary way of life which is dominated by technology. It reminds us that the digital world cannot replace all naturally occurring experiences and highlights the importance of taking abreak from our screens to enjoy nature in real life every once in a while.
